     ISSUES                          

 

Good Government

Many Americans are very concerned -- with good reason -- about the erosion of participatory democracy in this country. Who doesn’t feel discouraged and angry at the constant news of corruption and influence peddling in Washington? When lobbyists pay for votes by elected officials, we are robbed of our fundamental right as citizens to have our voices heard.

Voters in California’s 4th congressional district deserve a legislative representative who shares their values and who will put their interests first. In Congress, I will actively support legislation and policies such as bringing the war in Iraq to a quick and secure resolution, protection for pensions and Social Security, ensuring the government keeps its promise to our troops and veterans, access for all Americans to good, affordable health care and the preservation and stewardship of our natural environment.

 

 

Integrity

I want to be a part of a return to integrity, honesty and accountability in Congress.  As a graduate of the U.S. Air Force Academy, I still believe in its honor code: “We will not lie, cheat or steal, nor tolerate those who do.” It is shameful that a majority of voters do not trust their elected representatives and view politicians negatively. If I’m elected to office, I will list the name and special interest of every lobbyist I or any member of my staff meets with on my Congressional website. Security 

It takes both a strong military and accurate intelligence to keep our country secure. The brave men and women of our armed forces believe in our nation’s cause, and we must keep them well equipped and well-trained to defend us.  However, a strong military alone will not keep us safe. We need to regain the respect of our allies and the rest of the world so that we are able to utilize all aspects of problem solving in resolving conflict. 

We also need economic security. Currently, both our nation’s debt and our trade deficit are at record levels. When our nation’s financial standing is insecure, it impacts all of us. I will demand fiscal responsibility and work to end the dangerous cycle of borrow and spend currently in place in Congress. I will also help put an end to the outsourcing of American jobs and provide small businesses with the tools to encourage entrepreneurial innovation and job creation. You should be secure in your job and in the knowledge that the retirement system you’ve worked for and contributed to, will be there when you need it. 

 

 

Education

Education is the cornerstone to building a strong future for our country and we must commit to strengthening our schools. For example, we know that preschool and small classroom size leads to improved performance and graduation rates. We need to set a goal of ensuring that our children receive instruction by the best trained, most qualified teachers in the world. Providing opportunities for students to attain higher education will also be one of my priorities. Under the Bush administration, university and college fees have risen dramatically while federal and state grants and scholarships have decreased. Additionally, we need to return arts and vocational training to our nation’s education system. In order to remain competitive in a global economy, we must provide our youth with the tools to build our country’s future. 

 

 

 Healthcare and Quality of Life

Access to quality, affordable healthcare is critical. An illness should not cause anyone to lose their family’s life savings or cause bankruptcy. There are 43.6 million uninsured Americans. I will make addressing the current healthcare crisis a top priority. Protecting the environment is also vital in protecting the health and quality of life of all Americans, particularly our children. I will fight for clean air, clean water, and preservation of our natural resources. What we do today to protect our environment will have an impact on future generations of Californians.
